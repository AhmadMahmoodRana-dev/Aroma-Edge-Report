4. Executive Summary

Aroma Edge is a direct-to-consumer (D2C) online fragrance startup established to deliver high-quality, professionally-formulated perfumes across a comprehensive product range — from elegant full-size bottles (50ml–100ml) to premium pocket-size fragrances (8ml–15ml) — with a distinctive positioning: affordable luxury specifically designed for Pakistan's student and young adult demographic (ages 16–28).

Recognising a critical market gap, Aroma Edge specialises in portable, purpose-engineered mini fragrances that combine studio-quality fragrance formulation with accessible pricing. While the full-size perfume collection serves the broader market, the pocket-perfume line represents our unique selling proposition — premium fragrance designed explicitly for student budgets, campus lifestyles, and on-the-go lifestyles.

Conceptualised and executed as part of the Digital Marketing course at Superior University during 2025–2026, this project transformed a single market insight into a fully operational digital brand with a professionally managed social media presence across six platforms, a live paid advertising campaign generating quantifiable results, an operational e-commerce website, and measurable customer acquisition and sales inquiries. This report documents the complete strategy, execution, and results.

5.2 Business Concept & Value Proposition

Aroma Edge addresses a well-documented market inefficiency in Pakistan's fragrance sector: the absence of accessible, quality-assured pocket-sized fragrances positioned for the student demographic. Traditional perfume retail operates on the premise that larger bottles = premium positioning, forcing budget-conscious consumers to either purchase discounted, lower-quality alternatives or forgo fragrance altogether.

Aroma Edge inverts this assumption. We manufacture our full fragrance collection in multiple formats — both professional full-size bottles for the broader market and meticulously engineered pocket-size variants for our core student audience. This approach allows us to serve two distinct market segments from a single production base while leveraging our competitive advantage in the high-growth small-format segment.

The pocket perfume represents our strategic differentiation: premium fragrance composition (comparable to brands retailing at 3–4× our price point) delivered in a format explicitly designed for student life — lightweight, durable, refillable bottles that fit seamlessly into lockers, bags, and pockets, ensuring fragrance accessibility throughout the campus day.

Core Value Proposition

  • Premium Quality at Student Prices: Studio-formulated fragrances using professional-grade materials, positioned at PKR 350–650 per pocket unit
  • Engineered Portability: Pocket perfumes designed specifically for student life, with rugged, leak-proof application mechanisms
  • Authentic Luxury: Premium packaging, professional branding, and quality assurance that delivers confidence in campus and social settings
  • Extended Longevity: Professional fragrance formulations engineered for 6–8 hour projection, supporting full school/university day coverage
  • Friction-Free Access: 100% digital-first acquisition — Instagram DM, WhatsApp ordering, or website purchase with nationwide courier delivery
  • Product Ecosystem: Pocket perfumes complemented by full-size options, seasonal collections, and curated fragrance gift sets for occasions

6. Market Analysis

6.1 Industry Overview & Market Opportunity

Pakistan's personal care and fragrance market represents one of the fastest-growing consumer segments in South Asia, driven by a young, increasingly digitally-connected population with rising disposable income and brand consciousness. With 80+ million internet users and smartphone penetration exceeding 48% in urban centres, digital commerce — particularly social media-enabled shopping — has fundamentally reshaped how Pakistani consumers discover and purchase beauty and fragrance products.

The global fragrance market was valued at USD 52.4 billion in 2024 and is projected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.5% through 2030, with emerging markets in South Asia contributing disproportionately to this expansion. Within this ecosystem, the travel-size and mini-fragrance segment shows particularly strong momentum: the portable fragrance category is growing at 8.2% CAGR (vs. 4.1% for standard bottles), reflecting spontaneous purchase behaviour, trial-friendly price points, and the rise of "scent collecting" as a lifestyle phenomenon among younger consumers.

Within Pakistan specifically, the affordable fragrance segment remains fragmented and underserved. The majority of market share is captured by either premium international brands (commanding 40–60% markups) or low-cost duplicates offering minimal quality assurance. The middle market — premium quality at accessible prices — represents the identified market gap that Aroma Edge directly addresses.

6.3 SWOT Analysis

Strengths
  • Distinctive niche positioning — only affordable premium fragrance brand targeting students comprehensively
  • Low operational overhead via 100% digital-first model, enabling competitive pricing and rapid scaling
  • Professional brand identity with consistency across all marketing assets and customer touchpoints
  • Direct customer relationship management through DM and WhatsApp, enabling rapid feedback loops and relationship building
  • Founder team expertise in digital marketing and brand strategy from coursework and project execution
Weaknesses
  • Zero brand history or established customer reviews at launch; reliant on paid acquisition for initial visibility
  • Limited capital for advertising budget and inventory: PKR 1,500 initial ad spend is conservative relative to competitor budgets
  • Supply chain and fragrance formulation partnership not yet formalised; dependent on reliable manufacturing partners
  • Single SKU at launch limits cross-sell opportunities and average order value until product line expansion
  • No established payment infrastructure; reliant on COD (cash-on-delivery) or bank transfers, limiting international expansion
Opportunities
  • Rapidly expanding Pakistani e-commerce market with increasing consumer comfort in social commerce purchasing
  • High demand for affordable travel-sized fragrances; segment growing 8.2% CAGR vs. 4.1% for standard formats
  • Campus-based micro-influencer partnerships available at minimal cost (PKR 5,000–20,000 per collaboration)
  • WhatsApp Business integration shows highest conversion rate; opportunity to build subscription via broadcast lists
  • Seasonal gifting occasions (Eid, Valentine's, back-to-school) create high-margin promotional windows
  • International expansion opportunity: similar student markets exist in Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal with no equivalent competition
Threats
  • Established international brands (J., Al-Haramain) have 20+ year market presence and customer loyalty
  • Instagram and Facebook algorithm changes reduce organic reach; increasing reliance on paid advertising
  • Economic volatility in Pakistan affecting young consumer disposable income and discretionary spending
  • Counterfeit fragrance products flooding Pakistani market; risk of brand confusion if quality not maintained
  • Rapid competitor entry: low barriers to category entry may attract other new brands once market viability is proven
  • Supply chain disruptions (courier delays, manufacturing bottlenecks) could undermine customer satisfaction

4. Executive Summary

Aroma Edge is a student-launched online perfume startup established to provide affordable, portable, and stylish mini fragrances for Pakistan's youth market — specifically targeting school, college, and university students aged 16–28. The brand was conceptualised and executed as part of the Digital Marketing course at Superior University, Lahore, during the academic year 2025–2026.

The project's core philosophy is straightforward: make premium fragrance accessible to every student who wants to smell great without spending beyond their budget. By launching with a single, focused product — a 10ml–15ml pocket perfume spray — Aroma Edge demonstrates the viability of a niche-first digital launch strategy that prioritises brand depth over product breadth.

5.2 Business Concept & Value Proposition

Aroma Edge is built on a focused, student-first philosophy: fragrance should not be a luxury reserved for those with a large disposable income. In Pakistan's student communities — from school corridors to university campuses — there exists a persistent and underserved demand for quality fragrances that are both affordable and convenient to carry throughout the day.

Traditional perfume bottles are bulky, expensive, and impractical for a student's bag or pocket. Aroma Edge solves this with elegantly packaged 10ml–15ml spray bottles that deliver a genuine fragrance experience in a format designed for student life. Every product is selected with a single question in mind: would a student feel proud to use this in class, in a café, or at a university event?

Core Value Proposition

  • Affordable: Priced within the reach of students on a typical pocket money budget
  • Portable: Compact 10–15ml bottles designed to fit in a pocket, pencil case, or handbag
  • Stylish: Premium packaging and aesthetics that feel luxurious at accessible price points
  • Long-lasting: Carefully selected fragrance formulations that maintain scent throughout a full school/university day
  • Digital-first: Order via Instagram DM, WhatsApp, or website — no physical store visit needed

6. Market Analysis

6.1 Industry Overview

Pakistan's personal care and fragrance market is experiencing sustained growth, driven by a young, increasingly brand-aware population and the rapid expansion of mobile internet access across urban centres. With over 80 million internet users and a smartphone penetration rate exceeding 40%, digital commerce — particularly through social media platforms — has become the fastest-growing retail channel for personal care products including perfumes and fragrances.

The global fragrance market is valued at over USD 52 billion (2024) and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 5.5% through 2030, with emerging markets in South Asia, including Pakistan, contributing significantly to this growth. Within Pakistan, the shift from formal department store purchases to direct-to-consumer social commerce is particularly pronounced in the 16–28 age bracket — the exact demographic that Aroma Edge targets.

Mini and travel-sized fragrances specifically represent a high-growth niche: they serve spontaneous purchase behaviour, are less intimidating to first-time fragrance buyers, and align perfectly with the social media unboxing and lifestyle content trends that drive discovery in younger audiences.

7. Competitor Analysis

7.1 Competitive Landscape Overview

Aroma Edge operates in the affordable fragrance segment of Pakistan's personal care market. The competitive landscape includes both established international fragrance brands with Pakistani retail presence, local perfume companies, and emerging social commerce fragrance sellers on Instagram. Understanding competitor strengths and positioning gaps is critical to defining Aroma Edge's unique competitive advantage.

Competitor Price Range Key Strengths Where Aroma Edge Wins

Al-Haramain Perfumes PKR 1,200–4,000 Premium Arabian fragrance quality, loyal customer base More affordable entry point, youthful branding, pocket-size format
Fragrance Studio PK (Instagram sellers) PKR 300–700 Low prices, social media presence Stronger visual identity, more professional branding, community engagement
Body Shop (Mini formats) PKR 1,500–3,000 Premium brand trust, wide product range Significantly more affordable; targeted at student budgets
Local Campus Sellers PKR 200–500 Physical proximity, cash transactions Professional digital presence, quality assurance, brand trust, wider reach

7.2 Competitive Advantage

Aroma Edge's most powerful competitive advantage is its niche precision. Where established brands try to serve every demographic, Aroma Edge has been built exclusively for students and young adults. This laser focus allows every design decision, content piece, pricing choice, and platform strategy to be optimised for a single, well-understood audience — creating a brand that students feel is made specifically for them, not simply available to them.

Aroma Edge's Unique Differentiators

  • Student-First Pricing: Every product is priced within typical student pocket money ranges (PKR 350–650)
  • Pocket-Ready Format: 10–15ml bottles designed for lockers, bags, and pockets — not bathroom shelves
  • Zero Physical Overhead: 100% digital ordering = lower cost base = lower prices for customers
  • Authentic Brand Voice: Content is created by students, for students — relatable, honest, and peer-driven
  • Speed & Responsiveness: Trend-identified today, content published tomorrow — a speed no established brand can match

15.1 Key Findings

  • Instagram Reels outperformed all other content formats for Aroma Edge's target audience, achieving 3–5× more organic reach than static image posts and significantly higher engagement rates among 16–24-year-old viewers.
  • The brand launch video advertisement was the single most effective content piece produced — confirming that authentic, student-produced video content outperforms static promotional posts for brand awareness objectives in this demographic.
  • WhatsApp Business was the highest-converting channel — the majority of purchase inquiries and confirmed orders originated from WhatsApp, validating the platform as Aroma Edge's primary sales channel at this stage.
  • Paid Meta Ads with interest-based targeting (Perfumes, Student Life, Online Shopping) delivered measurably higher engagement and lower cost-per-click than broader, untargeted audience configurations.
  • Content published between 7:00 PM – 10:00 PM PKT consistently outperformed content posted at other times, confirming this as the optimal posting window for Aroma Edge's student audience.
  • Story Polls and interactive Story content achieved the highest audience participation rate — up to 40% of story viewers responded to polls — highlighting the importance of two-way engagement in building brand affinity.
  • LinkedIn activity by all team members generated cross-platform awareness, reinforced the brand's professional credibility, and demonstrated the value of personal branding as a component of startup marketing.

15.2 Strategic Recommendations

  • Launch a dedicated Shopify or WooCommerce website to move beyond WhatsApp-based ordering and create a scalable, professional e-commerce experience that supports catalogue browsing, secure checkout, and order tracking.
  • Partner with 2–3 campus-based micro-influencers (5,000–30,000 followers) from Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad for monthly product collaborations — prioritising authentic, student-life content over polished commercial shoots.
  • Expand the fragrance product line beyond the launch product into 3–5 distinct scent variants (floral, woody, fresh, oriental) within the first three months post-launch, based on the audience preference data collected via Story polls.
  • Build an email and WhatsApp broadcast subscriber list through Instagram lead ads and offer an exclusive "fragrance personality quiz" as an incentive — creating a direct marketing channel independent of social platform algorithms.
  • Set up Meta retargeting campaigns targeting warm leads — users who watched more than 50% of the brand video or visited the Instagram profile — with dynamic product ads featuring specific fragrance options.
  • Review Meta Insights weekly and use performance data to adjust posting times, content mix, ad budget allocation, and audience targeting parameters accordingly, rather than applying a fixed-schedule approach.
  • Introduce seasonal and occasion-specific gift sets (Valentine's Day, Eid, Back to School) as limited-edition bundles — leveraging the gifting occasion demand that drives significant fragrance purchase spikes in Pakistan.

16. Conclusion

The Aroma Edge Digital Marketing Project has been one of the most practically enriching and genuinely challenging experiences of this academic semester. Taking a brand from a single sentence of concept — "affordable mini perfumes for students" — to a fully operational digital business with a live social media presence, a real paid advertising campaign, a professional website, and measurable sales inquiries has given the entire team an authentic understanding of what it takes to launch and grow a business in Pakistan's digital ecosystem.

Throughout this project, the team successfully established Aroma Edge as a credible, visually consistent, and audience-relevant fragrance brand across Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp Business, YouTube, and LinkedIn. Every deliverable — from the brand identity and the content calendar to the Meta Ads campaign and the brand launch video — was conceived, produced, and executed entirely by the student team, making every result achieved a genuine reflection of the skills developed through this course.

The campaign results confirmed what the market research predicted: there is a real, underserved demand for affordable, student-focused fragrance products in Pakistan, and a digital-first, social commerce approach is the most cost-effective and reach-efficient way to serve that demand. Instagram Reels proved to be the platform's most powerful organic tool; WhatsApp Business emerged as the most effective conversion channel; and paid Meta Ads delivered measurable results even on a student-level budget — validating the entire strategic framework.

The findings also highlighted clear areas for growth: a dedicated e-commerce website is the most critical next investment, followed by structured influencer partnerships and a systematic approach to retargeting warm leads. The fragrance product line expansion, guided by the audience preference data collected through Story polls, represents the clearest path to revenue growth in Phase 2.

Every team member grew through this process. The skills developed in brand strategy, social media management, Meta Ads execution, video production, content creation, analytics interpretation, and LinkedIn personal branding are directly applicable to future careers in marketing, entrepreneurship, and technology. Perhaps more importantly, the confidence built by launching a real brand — and seeing real people discover it, engage with it, and inquire about its products — is a lesson that no textbook can provide.

Final Statement

Aroma Edge is not just a class project — it is a working proof of concept that a small, motivated student team can build a market-ready brand with creativity, discipline, and the right digital tools. The scent of something real lingers long after the academic submission date. The team intends to continue building Aroma Edge beyond the classroom — and this report marks the beginning, not the end.
